Pegasus Therapeutic Riding Centre, Sligo Ireland

An update: 27 September 2009

I have been involved with Pegasus since 2005 as one of three unpaid Directors. As a not-for-profit organisation with charitable status, it grew from only a few riders to more than 95 clients in 2008.

After completing TATA in 2004, I was able to donate 30,000 euros in installments to Pegasus over a four-year period and was also in a position to help with numerous other aspects of the centre.

There were many challenges though, including having to find new premises for 2009 and also difficulties in having enough funds to operate on an annual basis, including paying a salary to two qualified therapeutic riding instructors.

The lack of an indoor arena and the unpredictable weather in the northwest did not help matters either, resulting in hundreds of cancellations every year.

The recession in Ireland combined with the above problems forced us to make a decision in November 2008 to close down. This course of action was supported by Jill Carey, CEO of the Festina Lente Foundation in Bray, Wicklow.

Her advice was to develop a completely new business plan for Pegasus and then come back to it in a year or two year’s time. To use her words: “There will always be a need for these kinds of services”.

Petra who worked as a therapeutic riding instructor at Pegasus decided to go back to Slovakia and decided to walk with her horse Momo from Germany to her home country. She started her adventure in June 2009 and finished three months later.

Her plan is to start a facility in Slovakia, offering riding to adults and children with disability. Momo will be used as a therapeutic riding horse at the centre.
